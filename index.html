What is it for? AI agents reach tools — your files, search, a shell, secrets — through the MCP protocol. meshmcp is the firewall in front of them: every agent tool-call is gated by a WireGuard identity, decided deny-by-default, held for a human when it’s sensitive, and written into a ledger you can prove. Governed by construction

Not “trust us.” Prove it.

The chain you just verified is the whole idea: who did what is cryptographic, not a claim.

Identity

A key, not an account.

Every peer is a WireGuard public key on a private mesh — no logins, no cloud, no open ports. An unidentifiable caller is admitted to nothing.

Firewall

Deny by default.

Every drop, message, and cast is checked against a per-caller policy — the same kind that flagged your “password” note for co-sign. Allow the safe, hold the sensitive, deny the rest.

Ledger

Hash-chained memory.

Each record links to the last by hash, so any edit breaks the chain — exactly what you saw on the Proof tab. air film captures a window to replay and verify.
